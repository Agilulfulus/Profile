NALLANTLI

Welcome to my personal profile page. There's not all that much to see around here, but you are welcome to take a look around. You'll find here a nice brief overview of my work and interests, and perhaps something interesting to carry back home.

I am a Linguistics Major at the University of Florida, and will be graduating at the end of this year (2020). Despite majoring in Linguistics, I have a long and fortunate history with programming and Computer Science, which I continue to act in as a hobby along with my linguistics studies. In certain cases, the worlds do collide, but for the most part unless something calls for computational linguistics specifically they remain separate.

NAME

My real name is Benjamin R. Park, but on the internet I have taken up the username Nallantli [ˈnaːl.lãːn.tɬi] (or, Anglicized as [nəˈlãnt.li], or [nəˈlãn.(t)ʃi]), a calque of the Nahuatl postpositions nâl + tlân, meaning "to the other side" and "from; at" respectively, plus the additional tli nominalizer so that my name is indeed a regular noun. The meaning roughly translates to "(he/it) is from the other side (of something)." I like to consider this pseudonym to be a reflection of my two contrasting interests, the social aspects of learning and studying languages and the analytics of Linguistics and Computer Science. Additionally, I just think it sounds cool.

Many people who have spoken to me for an extended period of time on the internet (approximately two years ago I made the switch) refer to me as Aggy, an abbreviation of my former username Agilulfulus, taken as a dimunitive form of Italo Calvino's eponymous character Agilulf from the novel The Nonexistent Knight.
